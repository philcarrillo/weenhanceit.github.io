What WE've Learned

Our years of experience have taught us some valuable lessons which we can bring to your organization. We've seen a lot of changes in IT over our careers, but getting value from technology still depends on the skill of the people who are using it correctly to solve your business problem.

WE've learned that you can't provide solutions unless you understand the problem. So often we've seen the frustration that comes when the people with the business problem are forced to deal with the language of specifications and technical acronym spouters. WE want the end user to articulate the business problem and WE will ensure the correct technical solutions are delivered.

WE've learned that, in most projects, the objectives are changing as companies learn more about their market or as users create new ideas as the software evolves. WE think it is critical that the project methodolgy can react to these changes as quickly as the changes are taking place. Our methodology avoids the large software project syndrome of delivering ponderous solutions that are already out of date.

WE've learned that it is critical to break projects down into a series of working deliverables that users can begin working with. In cases where large scale roll out is required, WE recommend starting with a proof of concept, where a sub-set of data or functionality can be used to work out the kinks in operations or user interface.

Keeping projects small and agile, helps you stay in control of your software development. With a series of small deliverables you can check that the whole process is meeting your needs, you can change focus and you can reset priorites.
